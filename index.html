I want to build things that matter.

The areas that pull me most are creativity and design, healthcare, and productivity. Not because they're trendy industries, but because they directly affect how people live their lives.

I don't want to spend my life optimizing metrics that nobody cares about. I want to help create products that make people healthier, more creative, more capable, or more productive. I want technology to remove friction, unlock potential, and improve the quality of everyday life.

I want to combine technology, business, design, and artificial intelligence. I don't just want to build systems; I want to help decide what should be built and why. I want to understand the user, the problem, and the impact.

Money matters to me because I've sacrificed a lot to get where I am. I left my home country, spent years away from my family, navigated immigration challenges, studied while working, and built a life from scratch. Financial success represents freedom, security, and the ability to help the people I care about.

But money alone is not the goal.

When my life is over, I don't want my story to be that I worked, earned a paycheck, and disappeared. I want to leave something behind that improves people's lives in a meaningful way.

I want to create products that people remember, use, and benefit from. I want to contribute to a world that is healthier, more creative, more productive, and more human.

My goal is not simply to have a career in technology.

My goal is to build things that matter.
